Project Idea - Barbell Motion Tracking App

Overview

My idea is to create a mobile app that uses the phones inbuilt camera to watch you while you do compound lifts such as squats, bench-press or deadlifts.

In powerlifting, we often refer to lifts using a scale of difficulty called RPE (rating of perceived effort) where zero would be us at rest, 5 might be something that is somewhat difficult to lift, 7 being rather difficult, 9 being very difficult and 10 being our absolute maximum weight lift.

This is a scale that goes by feel, but it can also be interpreted with ease by a third party based on how fast the bar moves from the top of the lift to the bottom, then back up again. For example: If a squat with average weight takes 1 second down then 1.5 seconds up, but a very heavy lift takes 1 second down and 3 seconds up and comes close to stopping before the top, it would arguably be an RPE 9-10 lift.

This system works pretty well to get an idea of how difficult a training plan is, but provides no real data that can be analysed for real insight into your performance.

This app would use real time image recognition software to find the centre of mass of the barbell from a side perspective and then track and graph the motion of the bar over time, storing and later comparing the data from many individual lifts over many weeks. The user would only need to specify which lift is being performed and at what weight.

Motivation

Powerlifting is a passion of mine and the idea of using technology to help with my performance is something that really fascinates me. I regularly take personal training sessions with my coach to increase my strength and most of our performance over time is tracked by doing a session of max lifts, then training over several weeks and later doing more max lifts and comparing them.

The trouble with this is that I very much rely on my coach. When I train at the gym without him there, he relies on my ability to track what RPE my heaviest lifts were to gauge how much to increase my weight week by week.

Description

A new user would create an account where they input statistics such as their weight and height and what their current max squat, bench-press and deadlift lifts are. From there they would input a workout plan which includes some of these compound lifts.

Each time the user goes to the gym, they clip their phone into a stand and open up the app. They would select their workout plan, select the day of the workout they will be completing and get started.
As they progress through the lifts they will click record on the app, go to the rack and perform the lift, then click end recording.

Each lift will provide real time statistics on the travel path of the bar, the speed, the timing and whether your upward motion stopped at all along the way. This would be done with each consecutive lift, and at the end this data would be stored as a set for that particular day. Users might even be interested in reporting whether they were feeling energised or maybe sluggish that day to indicate to the app that they weren’t at peak performance. This is something we take into account with our RPE evaluation of a lift.

Once the user has completed many days’ worth of this with each of the compound movements the app would provide many forms of graphs and statistical analysis to provide the user with insight into their current performance and maybe even tips on what they need to focus on and what is progressing well.

Tools and Technologies

The implementation could be as simple as an app with local storage, but servers with databases should be strongly considered as the majority of users will wish to share their stats with friends or user their same account across multiple devices.

Xamarin is a great example of a framework that could be used to create an app that would be compatible with both Android and apple devices. It’s a reliable product provided by Microsoft and leverages .Net and C# which are very common and easy to learn programming languages.

The image tracking can likely leverage an online service initially while the app is in development but later on it would be ideal to write our own image tracking software that can run locally on each device.

Skills Required

Skills required would include Xamarin and .Net Framework development in C# for the mobile app.
An understanding of how to write API’s backed by databases in SQL will be required for the cloud/backend aspects of the project.

I am quite familiar with the .Net Framework in C# as well as SQL so these would be ideal technologies for me to make use of.

Outcome

This project will hopefully allow coaches and athletes to take a more data driven approach with benefits spanning many areas from strength gains to safety lifting technique.
